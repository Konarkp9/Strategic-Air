REVERSE-ENGINEERED ALGORITHM MECHANICS

1. Demand / Load Factor Model

Core finding: Demand is driven by business index, fare level, marketing spend, quality/reliability scores, cabin service, route competition, and fare sales. Load factors in this sim typically range from 50-70%.
  • Business Index effect: When BI went from 105 (Q3) to 99 (Q4), load factors dropped from 0.67 to 0.61 despite no fare change. ~1 point BI change = ~1% load factor shift.
  • Fare sensitivity: Raising fare from 35c to 36c (Q2) with reduced flights: LF went UP from 0.615 to 0.625. The sim appears to have modest fare elasticity - small fare increases (1-2c) don't kill demand if quality supports it.
  • Marketing impact: Despite massive marketing spend increases (Q5-Q6: $25K each promo+ad), load factors DROPPED. This suggests marketing has diminishing returns and doesn't override macro conditions and competition.
  • Fare Sales: 1-month and 2-month sales in Q3 boosted route LF by 5-10 percentage points (2A went from 0.60 to 0.68, 2B from 0.60 to 0.70). This is one of the strongest demand levers.
  • Quality/Reliability don't directly boost LF: Quality went from 64 to 89 over Q2-Q6, but LF fell from 0.625 to 0.577. Quality affects refund rate instead.

2. Revenue Model

Key formula: Net Revenue = Gross Revenue - Commissions (~9.1% fixed) - Refunds (variable, 2.5-9.7%)
  • Commission rate is fixed at ~9.1% of gross revenue across all quarters. This is not controllable.
  • Refund rate is the critical lever: Started at 9.7% (Q0), dropped to 4.7% (Q6). This directly correlates with Quality + Reliability. Going from Q65/R0.903 to Q89/R0.953 cut refunds by ~50%.
  • Refund reduction = pure profit: The refund reduction from Q0 to Q6 saved ~$136K per quarter in retained revenue. This is the single biggest profit driver.
  • Other Revenue (Cargo + Service Fees): Appeared in Q5 at $472K, grew to $512K in Q6. This is a massive revenue source that dwarfs the fare increase gains.

4. Quality Score Model

Quality is the #1 profit lever. It reduces refunds, boosts stock price, and is driven by: Training Budget, Compensation Policy, Maintenance Level, and Cabin/Food Service.
  • Training Budget: Q0=$1K→Quality 65. Q1=$3K→64. Q2=$7K→64. Q3=$12K→68. Q4=$12K→75. Q5=$20K→82. Q6=$20K→89. There's a LAG: spending takes 1-2 quarters to show results.
  • Maintenance Level: Moving from Lvl 1→Lvl 2 (Q2) improved reliability from 0.903→0.914. Lvl 2→Lvl 3 (Q5) pushed it to 0.943. Each level adds ~$3.5K/aircraft/quarter.
  • Compensation: Going to +3% above prevailing improved quality substantially. The benefit plateaus around 3-3.5%.
  • Quality grows cumulatively: Even holding spending steady at Q5-Q6 levels ($20K training, +3.2% comp, Lvl 3 maint), quality kept rising from 82→89. The sim has a momentum/accumulation factor.

5. Stock Price Model

Stock price tracks: Cumulative profit, quality trend, and possibly competitive rank. It does NOT simply follow quarterly profit.
  • Q4: Profit dropped to $35K but stock only fell $1 (from $24.22 to $23.27). The sim smooths stock price.
  • Q5: Huge jump ($23.27→$29.02, +25%) driven by profit spike ($133K) AND the fact that many competitors were losing money.
  • Group 1 had the highest stock ($35.35 in Q6) with consistent high profits AND early quality investment. The sim rewards CONSISTENCY of profit over total profit.

6. Fare Sales Mechanics

Fare Sales offer a temporary discount (reducing fare) but boost load factor significantly. Net effect varies by route.
  • 1-Month Sale appears to be ~5-7% fare discount with LF boost of 5-8 percentage points
  • 2-Month Sale appears stronger: 2B went from 0.60 LF to 0.70 in Q3
  • 3-Month Sale on 2D in Q6: LF went from 0.600 to 0.617 with 4 flights (up from 3)
  • Sales trigger refund reduction in some cases (likely the sim counts sale passengers as more satisfied)

STRATEGY ANALYSIS: WHAT WORKED & WHAT DIDN'T

What Worked Well

Cargo Service (Q5+): Added $472-512K/quarter in Other Revenue with relatively low marginal cost. This was the single biggest profit driver. Start cargo IMMEDIATELY in a new game.
Quality Investment Path: The steady ramp in training ($1K→$3K→$7K→$12K→$20K) and maintenance (Lvl1→2→3) drove quality from 65→89 and cut refunds by ~$136K/quarter.
Cabin Service (Q2+): At $1/passenger (~$36K/quarter cost), this improved quality perception and likely reduced refunds. Good ROI.
Conservative Fleet: Keeping 3 aircraft through most quarters avoided the losses that competitors like Aurora, Oura, and LUXBird suffered from aggressive expansion.
Debt Paydown: LT Loans dropped from $2.23M to $1.97M, saving ~$6K/quarter in interest. Cash grew to $1.7M providing strategic flexibility.

What Didn't Work / Missed Opportunities

Cargo delayed to Q5: Skipped it in Q1-Q2. If started in Q1, could have earned $1-2M more in cumulative Other Revenue by Q6.
Quality investment too slow: Competitors like Group 1 invested in quality earlier and more aggressively. They reached Quality 82 by Q4 while we were at 75.
Over-spending on marketing: $50K/quarter on promo+ad in Q5-Q6 with no clear load factor improvement. Marketing has severe diminishing returns past ~$15-20K total.
Not enough fare sales: Used sparingly in Q3 and Q5-Q6. Group 1 likely used them more aggressively to maintain high load factors.
Disposed owned aircraft Q4: Sold #1 TP-300 and leased #4. Leases cost $132K/quarter vs ~$48K depreciation+financing for owned. This increased costs by ~$84K/quarter.
Excess cash sitting idle: $1.7M in cash earning nothing. Should have been invested in CDs (5% annual = ~$21K/quarter) or used to pay down debt.

KNOWLEDGE GAPS & UNCERTAINTIES

Gap 1: Exact Demand Formula

We know the inputs (BI, fare, quality, marketing, competition, fare sales) but not the exact coefficients. We'd need controlled experiments (change only one variable at a time) to isolate each factor.

Experiment for new game: In Q1, change ONLY fare (keep everything else at defaults). In Q2, change ONLY marketing. This isolates each effect.

Gap 2: Optimal Marketing Spend Level

We know marketing has diminishing returns past ~$15-20K, but the exact curve is unknown. Group 1's marketing levels aren't visible to us (we only see our own marketing details).

Hypothesis: Optimal total marketing spend is $15-25K/quarter (including magazine, sales force). Beyond that, ROI goes negative.

Gap 3: Cargo Revenue Model

Cargo appeared as $472K in Q5, $512K in Q6. We don't know: what drives cargo revenue growth, what the cargo marketing budget was, how it scales with fleet size, or when it becomes profitable after initial investment.

Key insight: The Q2 message said cargo takes "several quarters" to become profitable. Given the $472K in Q5 (started Q2), the payback is ~3 quarters.

Gap 4: Aircraft Economics (Buy vs. Lease)

Owned aircraft: $2.6M purchase, $48K/qtr depreciation, builds equity. Leased: $132K/quarter, no equity. We don't know disposal values or optimal fleet composition. The Q4 swap (own→lease) cost us significantly.

Recommendation: In a new game, BUY aircraft if you can finance them. Leasing is expensive.

Gap 5: Stock Price Algorithm Weighting

Stock price seems to weigh: cumulative profit (30%?), quality trend (20%?), competitive rank (20%?), consistency (15%?), growth trajectory (15%?). But exact weights unknown.

Observation: Group 1 had lower Q6 profit than Aurora ($229K vs $300K) but higher stock ($35.35 vs $31.78). Consistency is heavily rewarded.

Gap 6: Fare Sales Exact Discount Amount

We see the LF boost from fare sales but not the exact fare reduction. Need to calculate: (Gross Revenue / RPM) during sale quarters vs. non-sale to back out the implicit discount.

Universal Rules for Any Strategy

The algorithm rewards these behaviors regardless of strategy:
  • Start cargo immediately - it's worth $400-500K/quarter by Q5
  • Never skip quality investment - refund reduction is the #1 profit lever
  • Use fuel contracts (50/50) when prices are rising to hedge
  • Buy CDs with excess cash - free money at 4-5% annual
  • Don't over-market - $15-20K total is the sweet spot
  • Use fare sales on routes with LF < 60% to boost demand
  • Accept baggage/service fees when offered - additional revenue stream
  • Maintain 340+ miles under max daily mileage to avoid FAA fines
  • Stock dividends when offered boost stock price
  • Keep fleet lean - don't add aircraft unless LF is consistently above 65-70%
