KEY FINDINGS: What the ML Models Reveal

Finding #1: Reliability is the #1 predictor of profit across ALL companies (Combined Score: 0.905, r=+0.866). Airlines with higher reliability consistently earned more profit. This makes sense: reliability reduces refunds (r=-1.000 between reliability and refund rate).
Finding #2: Quality → Refund Rate is the strongest causal mechanism (r=-0.949). Every point of quality reduces the refund rate, directly boosting net revenue. Training budget is the primary driver of quality (r=+0.909).
Finding #3: Compensation % has a NEGATIVE correlation with profit across all companies (r=-0.801). Oura paid 20% above prevailing wages and lost massively. The sweet spot appears to be 0-3.2%. Overpaying destroys value.
Finding #4: More aircraft = less profit (r=-0.522 across all companies). The simulation punishes excess capacity. The most profitable companies (Group 1) stayed at 3 aircraft.
Finding #5: Marketing has NEGATIVE correlation with load factor (r=-0.687 for Strategic Air). This is counter-intuitive but suggests massive diminishing returns. Or it means you increased marketing BECAUSE load factors were dropping (reverse causality).
Finding #6: Other Revenue (Cargo) is the single strongest profit predictor for Strategic Air (r=+0.925). Cargo/service fee revenue was the #1 driver of profit improvement.

MODEL LIMITATIONS & CAVEATS

Small sample size: 42 observations (all companies) and only 7 for Strategic Air detail. Correlations may be spurious, especially with many features. The R² values are likely overfitted on the training data.
Multicollinearity: Many decisions were changed simultaneously (e.g., training + maintenance + fare all increased over time). The models can't perfectly isolate individual effects. The "quarter" variable captures this trend and dominates some models.
Reverse causality possible: Marketing negatively correlating with load factor might mean "you spent more marketing BECAUSE LF was dropping" rather than "marketing causes LF to drop."
Most trustworthy findings (consistent across all methods): Reliability → Profit (+), Quality → Refund Rate (−), Training → Quality (+), Maintenance → Reliability (+), Excess Aircraft → Profit (−), Other Revenue → Profit (+).
